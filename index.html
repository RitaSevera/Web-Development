Madeira

A privileged geographical position, a subtropical climate, unique landscapes and outstanding cultural traditions: discover Madeira's charms. Madeira Island, located in the Atlantic Ocean, is a natural paradise that combines stunning landscapes, a mild climate and a rich cultural heritage. Known as "the Pearl of the Atlantic", Madeira captivates the hearts of visitors with its exuberant beauty and diversity.

Porto Santo

From the paradisiacal beach to the historical heritage and geological exuberance, discover this secret hidden in the ocean. To visit Porto Santo is to discover a golden island, famous for its nine-kilometer sandy beach. The smallest inhabited island in the Madeira Archipelago is just an hour and a half's flight from Lisbon, in the far southwest of Europe, 500 km from the African coast and 1000 km from the European mainland.

Ilhas Desertas

The Desertas Islands Nature Reserve is made up of Deserta Grande, Ilhéu Chão and Bugio. Its protected status was decided in 1990 due to the urgent need to protect the world's rarest monk seal (Monachus monachus). Located 30 km south-east of Madeira, the reserve covers, in addition to these three islands, an area of 9672 hectares which includes the underwater area up to Ponta de São Lourenço, to a depth of 150m.
